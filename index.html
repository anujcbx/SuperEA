What is AI?
Artificial Intelligence is technology that can understand, learn, and perform tasks typically requiring human intelligence. For EAs, think of it as a super-smart assistant that handles routine work so you can focus on strategy.

Key insight: AI isn't about replacing you—it's about amplifying your value. You become the strategic partner, not the administrative workhorse.

💡
Why This Matters for EAs
  • Time Savings: 5-10 hours per week on routine tasks
  • Quality Improvement: Professional emails, comprehensive reports, better-organized data
  • Strategic Value: Move from administrative support to strategic partnership
  • Error Reduction: 90% fewer mistakes in documentation and follow-ups
  • Better Work-Life Balance: Less stress, more meaningful work

Prompt Library (Copy & Paste)
Ready-to-use prompts for all your daily tasks. Click to copy.
📧 Prompt 1: Draft Executive Response
Subject: [INSERT EMAIL SUBJECT] Incoming Email: [PASTE THE EMAIL YOU NEED TO RESPOND TO] Please draft a professional response from [Executive Name]. The tone should be [formal/friendly/warm]. Keep it concise (2-3 paragraphs). Include: - Acknowledgment of their request - Clear answer or next steps - Professional closing Make it sound like [his/her] voice.
📧 Prompt 2: Meeting Request Email
Draft a professional meeting request email to [Recipient Name] with these details: - Topic: [e.g., "Quarterly Planning Discussion"] - Purpose: [e.g., "Review Q3 performance and Q4 goals"] - Preferred times: [e.g., "Tuesday or Wednesday, 2-4 PM EST"] - Duration: [e.g., "30 minutes"] - Location: [e.g., "Virtual via Zoom"] Make it concise and professional.
📧 Prompt 3: Change Tone
Please rewrite this email in a [more formal/friendlier/more urgent] tone: [PASTE THE DRAFT EMAIL] Keep the same content but adjust the language and style.
📝 Prompt 1: Generate Meeting Minutes
Please generate professional Minutes of Meeting from this transcription: [PASTE TRANSCRIPT OR MEETING NOTES] Include: - Meeting title, date, attendees, duration - Key topics discussed (organized by agenda item) - Decisions made - Action items (with owner and deadline) - Next meeting date Use a professional format.
📝 Prompt 2: Extract Action Items
From this meeting transcript, extract all action items: [PASTE TRANSCRIPT] For each action item, identify: - What needs to be done - Who is responsible - Deadline (if mentioned) - Priority level Format as a numbered list.
📝 Prompt 3: Meeting Summary Email
Based on this meeting transcript, draft an email summary to send to participants: [PASTE TRANSCRIPT] Include: - Brief recap of key topics - Decisions made - Action items (list who did what) - Next steps - Next meeting date Keep it concise (under 300 words) and professional.
🔍 Prompt 1: Competitive Analysis (Use Perplexity)
Research and provide current information on competitors in the [INDUSTRY]: For each of the top 3-5 competitors to [OUR COMPANY]: - Company name and brief description - Recent news, funding, or major announcements (last 6 months) - Market position and key differentiators - Pricing model (if public) - Key leadership changes - Recent product launches Include sources for all information.
🔍 Prompt 2: Industry News Summary
What are the top 5 news stories in [INDUSTRY] this month that would impact [OUR COMPANY]? For each story, include: - Headline - What happened (2-3 sentences) - Why it matters for us - Recommended action (if any) Include publication and date.
🔍 Prompt 3: Event Research
Research upcoming [INDUSTRY] conferences and events for [CALENDAR YEAR]. For each major event, provide: - Event name and dates - Location - Expected attendance - Registration costs - Key speakers - Relevance to our business - Registration link Include at least 5-10 major events.
✈️ Prompt 1: Complete Travel Itinerary
Please create a detailed 3-day itinerary for [Executive Name] traveling to [CITY]. Trip details: - Dates: [CHECK-IN] to [CHECK-OUT] - Purpose: [Business/Personal/Conference] - Key meetings/events: [LIST] - Preferences: [e.g., high-end hotel, near business district] Include: - Recommended hotels (3-5 options with links) - Flight options - Ground transport - Restaurant recommendations - Emergency contacts Format as a complete guide.
✈️ Prompt 2: Pre-Travel Checklist
Create a pre-travel checklist for [Executive Name] traveling to [COUNTRY/CITY] from [DATE] to [DATE]. Include: - Documents needed (passport, visa, etc.) - Confirmations needed - What to pack (for climate/business context) - Travel app recommendations - Emergency contacts - Expense tracking reminders Organize by urgency (immediate, 1 week before, day before, day of travel).

Prompt Chaining: Tools Working Together

What it is: Using the output from one AI tool as input for another to create sophisticated workflows.

Example: Competitive Intelligence Report

  1. Step 1 (Perplexity): "Research the top 3 competitors. For each, find: recent news, funding, product launches, market position."
  2. Step 2 (Claude): "Analyze this competitor data. Identify: threats, opportunities, our advantages. Provide strategic recommendations."
  3. Step 3 (ChatGPT): "Format this into a professional Board report with executive summary, landscape, recommendations, action items."
  4. Step 4 (Canva AI): "Create 5-6 presentation slides with key insights from this report."

Result: Complete market intelligence → Board presentation in 60 minutes (vs. 8+ hours manually)

Best Practices & Tips
Master the fundamentals for consistent, reliable results.
✅
Do's: What Makes AI Work For You
  • Be Specific: The more detailed your prompt, the better the output.
  • Provide Examples: If you have a sample format, include it. AI learns from examples.
  • Iterate: First draft is rarely perfect. Ask for revisions and tone changes.
  • Use Chaining: Research (Perplexity) → Analysis (Claude) → Formatting (ChatGPT)
  • Always Proofread: AI makes mistakes. Review before sending, especially executive communications.
  • Customize Output: Personalize AI drafts with your executive's unique voice.
  • Document Prompts: Save prompts that work. Build your reusable library.
❌
Don'ts: Common Mistakes to Avoid
  • Don't Trust Blindly: Always fact-check, especially dates and numbers.
  • Don't Share Confidential Data: Never paste sensitive company info into public AI tools.
  • Don't Use Vague Prompts: "Write an email" gives generic results. Be specific.
  • Don't Skip Review: AI-drafted emails that sound robotic hurt relationships.
  • Don't Assume Current Info: ChatGPT/Claude have knowledge cutoffs. Use Perplexity for latest information.
  • Don't Forget Security: Use enterprise versions for sensitive work. Check data privacy policies.
🔒
Data Privacy & Security
  • 🛡️ Use Enterprise Versions: ChatGPT Business, Claude Team (data isn't used for training)
  • 🚫 Never Share: Passwords, API keys, client names, confidential data, financial details
  • 🔐 Anonymize Data: Replace names with [NAME], numbers with [AMOUNT]
  • 📋 Check Terms of Service: Understand how each tool uses your data
  • 🔄 Review Company Policy: Check if your company has AI tool approval list
